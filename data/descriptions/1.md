# Areca Catechu Flower (Pakku Flower) – The Golden Offering of Tradition

The Areca Catechu flower, revered as *Pakku Poo* or *Kamuku Poo*, is not merely a flower; it is a sacred vessel of divine energy. In the vast tapestry of Indian Vedic traditions, this flower holds a unique status—it is one of the few botanical offerings believed to possess the power to purify the aura of a space instantly. ## Spiritual Significance & Uses
* **Ganapathi Homam:** It is the primary offering for Lord Ganesha. The unique scent and structure are said to attract the deity's blessings for removing obstacles.
* **Naga Dosha Pariharam:** In many traditions, worshiping the Serpent Gods (Nagas) is incomplete without this flower.
* **Kalasam Decoration:** Its cascading nature mimics a golden waterfall. Placing it atop the Purna Kumbham or Kalasam invites overflowing wealth into the home.

## Care Instructions
To maintain its divine glow:
1.  **Hydration:** Sprinkle cool water on the strands every 3 hours.
2.  **Storage:** Wrap the base in a wet cotton cloth and store it in the vegetable compartment of your fridge if using the next day.
3.  **Handling:** Handle gently by the main stem to prevent the delicate strands from shedding.
